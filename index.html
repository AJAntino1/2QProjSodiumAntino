What is Coronavirus?

Coronavirus is an infectious disease caused by the SARS-Cov-2 virus. The first known case is from Wuhan, China in December 2019. It has spread worldwide leading to an ongoing pandemic. Coronaviruses are a group of viruses covered in little protein spikes that look like a crown or "corona" in Latin. There are a lot of coronaviruses record in the history including the SARS 2003 virus which also first detected in China. It also caused an outbreak that infected a total 8,098 people worldwide which 774 people died. Compared to the new Covid-19 strain, SARS was much more fatal and deadlier but less infectious compared to Covid-19.

What are the symptoms and effects of Covid-19?

Symptoms of Covid-19 often include fever, cough, headache, fatigue, difficulty in breathing, lost of smell and taste, sore throat, tiredness, and chest pain

Symptoms begin one to fourteen days after exposure to the virus. Most people develop mild to moderate symptoms, while some develop severe symptoms like dyspnea or hypoxia and few patients suffer critical symptoms like respiratory failure, shock, or multiorgan dysfunction.

Some of the people who are infected with the virus remain asymptomatic and do not develop noticeable symptoms, but can still spread the disease. Some patients continue to experience a range of effects of Covid-19 months after recovery and damage to organs has been observed. Many studies are underway to further investigate the long term effects of Covid-19.
