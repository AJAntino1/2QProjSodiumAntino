The Outbreak of Covid-19

On December 31 2019, the world was informed about the cases of pneumonia of unknown cause in Wuhan City on China. A novel coronavirus was identified as the cause by Chinese authorities on 7 January 2020 and was temporarily named “2019-nCoV”.

On January 30 2020, WHO declared nCov outbreak as an international concern.

On March 22 2020, the cases of the Covid-19 skyrocket and on April 4 2020 the Covid-19 cases reached over 1 million.

On May 2020, the Beta variant of Covid-19 started to emerged from South Africa causing a surge on the Covid-19 cases.

On June 27 2020, the Covid-19 reached 10 million cases worldwide and its rapidly spreading across the United States of America, Brazil, Russia, India, and around the world.

On September 2020, the Alpha variant of the Covid-19 was detected and it became a variant of concern because of its mutation on its spike protein which made it more transmissible than the original virus of Covid-19 and also the vaccine candidates started their research and clinical development of the vaccines for Covid-19 virus.

On October 2020, the Delta variant of the Covid-19 was detected at India and it was more infectious and deadly compared on the other variants of Covid-19

On November 2020, the Gamma variant of the Covid-19 disease was discovered and it spreads faster compared than the other variants but has less severe effects on the infected and also the first Covid-19 vaccination was started by United Kingdom and followed up by the other countries later.

On January 25 2021, the Covid-19 virus has infected 100 million people worldwide and its further spreading across the world.

On November 9 2021, the first case of Omicron Variant was reported from South Africa and it has been tested that it multiplies 50 times faster compared than the Delta variant but causes less severe effects.

Today March 2, 2022, over 56.2% of people worldwide are vaccinated and the total Covid-19 cases is 437 million with 5.96 million deaths.
